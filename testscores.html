Breaking down state assessments

An analysis of Nebraska State Assessment scores and free and reduced lunch percentages

By Aidan Connolly

Reading and math proficiency increased statewide this year according data released by the Nebraska Department of Education.

Scores from the 2016 Nebraska State Assessments showed 82 percent students were proficient in reading, an increase from 80 percent 2015. Math proficiency reached 73 percent, a percentage point increase from last year.

The Nebraska State Assessments test students in each of the state's 1,130 public schools. Math and reading tests are given to students in third through eighth and 11th grades.

While the statewide average is on the rise, some schools failed to meet standards. With 289 students tested in Winnebago Public Schools, less than 25 percent were proficient in math and only 46 percent were proficient in reading.

Walthill Public Schools had less than 23 percent proficient in math and less than 37 percent proficient in reading with 198 students tested.

The Nebraska Department of Education also released free and reduced lunch counts for the 2016-2017 school year. Schools with higher percentages of students on free or reduced lunch often had lower test scores.

Elkhorn Public Schools had both the highest proficiency scores and the lowest percentage of students on free and reduced lunch. It had a math index score of 669 and a reading score of 680, both out of a possible 700. Only 8 percent of students were on free or reduced lunch.

Winnebago Public Schools had the lowest index scores with math at 170 and reading at 323. It also had the second highest free and reduced lunch percentage with 76 percent.
